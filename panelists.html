Details

The panel discussion will take place from 13:30 to 14:30 and will run for a total of 60 minutes

The session will begin with a 15-minute introduction segment, during which each panellist will have the opportunity to introduce themselves and their organisation. Panellists may also choose to give a brief introductory presentation if they wish.

This will be followed by 30 minutes of moderated discussion, led by the panel chair. The discussion will focus on the topics and questions outlined below, while also allowing flexibility for panellists to explore related issues and emerging themes relevant to the workshop objectives.

The final 15 minutes will be dedicated to key takeaways, concluding remarks, and potential follow-up actions. This segment will provide an opportunity to summarise the main points of discussion and identify priorities for future collaboration and engagement.

Audience participation will be encouraged throughout the session to promote an interactive and engaging exchange of ideas. The challenges, opportunities, insights, and recommendations arising from the discussion will be captured in a white paper (at the bottom of the page). This document will serve as a formal record of the session outcomes and provide a valuable reference for both current and future members of the community.

Topics and questons

1. The Role of Open Source in Advancing Surgical, Biomedical, and Medical Robotics

How open-source software is transforming surgical robotics, medical imaging, and digital health?.

What are the most significant benefits of open-source approaches for research, innovation, and clinical translation?

What barriers continue to limit the adoption of open-source technologies in healthcare organisations and industry?

What lessons can be learned from successful open-source initiatives such as 3D Slicer, MONAI, OpenCV, Isaac Lab, ROS, etc?

2. Future Trends and the Sustainability of Open-Source Ecosystems

How can open-source platforms accelerate innovation and unlock greater clinical and commercial value?

What is the key barrier to scaling open-source healthcare technologies, and how can we address it collectively?

How might open-source technologies disrupt existing healthcare and medical technology markets?

Which emerging technologies are likely to have the greatest impact on healthcare and medical robotics over the next decade?

How might open-source technologies disrupt existing healthcare and medical technology markets?

3. AI, Open Source, and Regulatory Challenges in Healthcare

How can open-source software and AI models be safely deployed within regulated healthcare environments?

What examples exist of open-source AI frameworks being used in clinical studies, trials, or approved healthcare products?

What lessons can be learned from successful clinical translation and regulatory approval pathways?

4. Emerging Opportunities, Practical Guidance, and Global Perspectives

How are new software and hardware technologies changing the way healthcare and medical robotics solutions are developed and deployed?

What opportunities and challenges arise from collaborations between academia, healthcare providers, industry, and open-source communities?

How are geopolitical strategies influencing the development of open-source software, AI models, datasets, and digital infrastructure?

What should the open-source healthcare community prioritise over the next five years?

White Paper

Our immediate goal is to develop this white paper as a community-driven effort, and we invite all workshop participants to contribute their expertise, perspectives, and experiences. Together, we can create a valuable resource that reflects the collective knowledge of the field.

The topics covered in this white paper are both timely and important, with the potential to shape the future of our community. By sharing insights, lessons learned, and diverse viewpoints, we can help define best practices, identify key challenges, and highlight opportunities for future research, development, and collaboration.

Looking beyond the workshop, we aim to further refine the document and identify an appropriate publication venue to maximize its visibility and impact. Our goal is for this white paper to become a shared reference for researchers, developers, practitioners, and stakeholders, capturing the current state of the art while helping guide future directions.

Your participation is essential to the success of this initiative. We encourage you to share ideas, suggest additions, and contribute examples that can strengthen the document and increase its value to the wider community.
