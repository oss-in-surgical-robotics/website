Poster Submission Guidelines

📢 We invite attendees to share their work through poster presentations at the From Code to Care: Challenges, Success Stories, and the Future Directions of Open-Source Development in Medical Robotics workshop. Your innovative contributions are warmly welcomed.

📝 Abstract Templates

Word and LaTeX templates are available in the posters/templates folder of our repository. You can also make use of our Overleaf project (read access for anyone with the link).

🧾 Zenodo DOIs for accepted posters and two-page abstracts

For authors who wish to make their work publicly available, we kindly ask that you upload your posters to Zenodo. This provides a formal citation option for your work, such as a DOI.

  1. Create a new Zenodo submission at https://zenodo.org/uploads/new.
  2. Drag and drop your PDF poster.
  3. If you already have a DOI for this upload, add it. Otherwise, select No and Zenodo will automatically create one for your poster.
  4. Select resource type “Poster” and add the title, creators (names, ORCID, affiliation, role), and a description (the abstract of your poster).
  5. Select a licence for your poster.
  6. Add the conference details:

    Title: The 18th Hamlyn Symposium on Medical Robotics

    Acronym: HSMR26

    Place: Royal Geographical Society

    Dates: 26th June 2026

    Website: Hamlyn Symposium event page

    Session: From Code to Care: Challenges, Success Stories, and the Future Directions of Open-Source development in Medical Robotics

  7. Feel free to fill in any additional Zenodo fields that apply to your submission: recommended information, funding, alternative identifiers, related work, references, publishing information, and domain-specific fields.
  8. Please share your Zenodo DOI in the email thread once submitted.
  9. Many thanks for your submission — please don’t hesitate to reach out to Miguel Xochicale (m.xochicale@ucl.ac.uk) if there is anything we can help with.

📐 Poster Size and Allocation

We have an allocation of 8 poster boards located in the Marquee. Each board is double sided, allowing for a maximum of 16 A0 portrait posters. The physical poster must comply with the A0 portrait standard:

  • Maximum height: 118.9 cm (46.8 inches)
  • Maximum width: 84.1 cm (33.1 inches)

A1 or A2 size is also acceptable if that is a more portable option for you. The poster should not be laminated. Mounting materials will be provided at the poster board onsite.

All poster boards will be labelled with the workshop name and a poster number. If you are a presenter, please come to the registration desk at 08:30 and one of our volunteers will direct you to the correct board to set up your poster.

🎤 Poster Spotlights

We have a Spotlight session for posters at 12:00. Please prepare a one-minute presentation of your poster in any format that suits you — this could be simply projecting your poster, using slides with videos or demos, or even bringing portable hardware if possible. Due to a tight schedule, presentations must be strictly limited to one minute. You’ll have further time during lunch and at the poster marquee to discuss your work in more detail.
