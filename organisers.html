Organising Committee

Miguel Xochicale
Lead Organiser
University College London
Miguel is a Senior Research Engineer at University College London, where he leads pioneering work in data-centric AI tools for medical imaging, MedTech, SurgTech, biomechanics, and clinical translation, all using open-source software. His work focuses on driving innovation and delivering impact in several critical areas: Real-time AI for surgery, eye movement disorders, and echocardiography, Sensor fusion data integrating wearable trackers with medical imaging, Generative models for fetal imaging, and Child-robot interaction in low-resource countries. By harnessing these cutting-edge technologies, Miguel is dedicated to transforming healthcare through AI and making a lasting impact on patient care and medical research.

Junichi Tokuda
Co-Organiser
Brigham and Women’s Hospital and Harvard Medical School
Junichi is a research scientist specializing in biomedical engineering at Brigham and Women’s Hospital in Boston, MA, USA. His research focuses on image-guided and robot-assisted interventions, particularly MRI-guided biopsy and ablation. He has extensive experience in developing and clinically translating open-source software tools to support these procedures. He is also an Associate Professor of Radiology at Harvard Medical School.
Reza Haqshenas
Co-Organiser
University College London
Reza is Assistant Professor in Therapeutic Ultrasound, UCL Mechanical Engineering. His research interests lie in the interface between physical acoustics and biological physics, with applications to pharmaceutical, health and biomedical problems. Reza is interested in leveraging high-performance computing in therapeutic ultrasound and developing efficient numerical methods for solving problems in physical acoustics. He is a lead developer of OptimUS, an open-source, fast full-wave acoustic solver for treatment planning for focused ultrasound therapy.
